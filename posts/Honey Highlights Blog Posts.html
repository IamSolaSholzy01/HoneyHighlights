5 TOOLS AND 5 PRODUCTS YOU SHOULD HAVE AS A MAKEUP BEGINNER

 

 

Starting to pick interest in makeup? Do you love and appreciate that great and flawless look you see on celebrities and people when they use makeup? Have you tried learning to do makeup and you seem to be missing that all-important touch? Well I want to assure you that it is possible for you, yes you, to get that exquisite touch you're looking for. And, it's not rocket science, it's not even difficult. To accomplish that look you're looking for, it is important, in fact, imperative, that you have the right tools.

It is not only possible, but very common to find that individuals use the wrong tools for applying their products. While each makeup tool is designed for a specific purpose, at times it could be a little intimidating, especially to those just starting, to identify the right use and the right way since a single tool could serve multiple purposes, and some may even be used in the absence of another.

How then can you know the right tool to use? Come along with me, and let's learn about five tools and five products you must have as a makeup beginner.

TOOLS

Powder Brush

A powder brush is usually a fluffy and round shaped brush designed in such a way that blends powder makeup as you apply it, such that some refer to it as a big blending brush. There are some angled or slant shaped brushes, and these are better used for applying blush and bronze, as well as, for highlighting specific parts of the face.

To use:

Swirl the brush on top of the powder, tap it off to get rid of excess powder, and then apply it from the center of the face to the outer part of the face.

Foundation brush/beauty sponge

It is a round, domed shaped densely packed brush which allows blending of foundation as you brush. When using this brush don�t be too heavy handed instead build up the amount of product you would like to apply instead of brushing all at once. A sponge is ideal for a flawless and natural foundation application; it is also very easy to use.

How to use:

Start from the center of the face and work to the outward corner in circular motions, for a beauty sponge make sure its damp before you use.

Blending Brush

A blending brush is a loose dome shaped brush perfect for blending both wet and dry products. It can be used for applying eye shadow which blends colors effortlessly on the eye lid and crease. It can also be used to blend concealer to the skin; you can have as many blending brushes as you want depending on the purpose you want it to serve.

How to use:

In sweeping movement, glide brush across lid to apply eye color.

Eyebrow Brush

This brush is commonly referred to as an angled brush, it is slanted linear brush used to both fill and highlight brows. This brush helps to create precise lines and noticeable definition while applying your concealer.

Spoolie / Mascara Wand

It is a long brush with soft and spiral bristles. They are used for brushing and blending brow fillers. They can also be used to remove clumps following mascara application.

 

 

Now that you know these basic tools you might be wondering what kind of products can they be used for and which is most important, all these questions will be answered below as I explain five products that you should have as a makeup beginner.

PRODUCTS

Foundation

A foundation is a liquid applied to the face to create an even, uniform color to the complexion. It covers flaws and gives the desired finish matte or dewy look depending on what you want. Foundations work best when matched with the skin tone.

 

Concealer

Concealers could also be known as a color corrector, it is a type of cosmetics used to mask dark circles, age spots, large pores and other blemish visible to the skin, it can also be used to highlight T zone areas on your face and also you brow areas. It covers area that you find imperfect, however it cannot act alone.

A Powder

It could be compact or loose depending on your choice; it also works best when it�s the same shade with your skin tone and is usually applied right after a foundation. It is important to know that to avoid looking cakey, use only one coat of powder on your face and avoid adding excess powder.

 

A Brow Pencil / Brow Gel

This is used to fill the brows and make it look fuller and a little bit darker, a brow pencil should be used when your brows are more defined and full but a brow gel should be used if your brows are sparse and you need them to visible.

 

A Lipstick / Lip gloss

Depending on your choice and occasion, you could use a lip-gloss for a casual outing this prevents your lips from cracking and looking dry but a lipstick on the other hand could give you a bold look especially if you have an event or an occasion depending on your preference.

These basic tools and products can be found easily in your local market depending on your choice and budget, once you have them you are good to go and ready to slay that beautiful look you want any time any day.
